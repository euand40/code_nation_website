About Me

My Story

Throughout the past five years of my life my experience has been purely academic. This journey first started after highschool when I decided to enrole on a Level 3 BTEC in Construction and the Built Environment. On this course I did very well and enjoyed my experience, so after taking the course I decided to go to University studying Quantity Surveying. Again through hard work and determination I did very well, however I didn't enjoy my experience at University all that well.

This made me evaluate my life choices and wonder if construction was really for me. To help aid this decision the COVID19 pandemic occured just as I had graduated, leaving me in competition with other more experienced construction professionals. This was a turning point for me. While looking and applying to Construction jobs, I felt like none of the jobs I had looked at or applied for resonated with me, I decided my heart wasn't in it and chose to look elsewhere. This is where IT/Technology came in.

I have always been passionate about technology. I have a basic knowledge of computer hardware, love to game and have always been interested in how games and software are made. However, I always thought that if I took a degree in a specific subject. I wouldn't have the option to do something else. So when my work coach for universal credit told me about a three week coding course with Code Nation that they could fund, I was first of all shocked that I was given the opportunity to do something like that and eager to learn about something I had always been passionate about. Which leads us to the now, where I am continuing to pursue what I am passionate about in hopes I can kickstart a career in the IT/Technology sector.

Moral of my story, to anyone who is stuck with what they should do for a career is to follow what you are passionate about. Listen and understand the signs you are telling yourself. If you are following a career path or any path for that matter where you feel miserable or you are telling others "I wish I had done that with my life" go and do it! But be smart about your choices, if you are thinking of quitting a stable job for a new career path, ease into it first and be absolutely sure it is what you want. If anything use the qualifications/experience gained from your previous role as a back up if things go South.
